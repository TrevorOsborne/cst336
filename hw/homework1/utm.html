Unmanned Aircraft System Traffic Management (UTM)


UTM is a new system that the U.S. federal government has had to compile quickly to deal with the recent boom in private and commercial UAV activity. It’s a traffic management ecosystem that connects all UAVs in a large operational are. The basic idea is that a large amount of commercial UAV activity can be incorporated into this UTM using established flight plans. Most of these UAV operations can be preplanned and stick to a predetermined flight path/schedule and still achieve the user’s desired purpose.

With a well-organized UTM, every new flight plan entry could be queued and ensured separation from other scheduled UAV flights. This UTM could also be collaborated with the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) traffic management unit. UAV flight plans could be augmented to avoid any interference with aircraft in the National Air Space (Federal Aviation Administration, 2017). UTM is an interesting type of UAS that could be an effective means to organize the network of most UAV activity.
